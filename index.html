Key Findings

THE MARKET IS
STRUCTURALLY BROKEN
IN SPECIFIC PLACES

Sports drives 89% of Kalshi's fee revenue. Sports markets have a median spread of 100%. These facts coexist because the volume is concentrated in a handful of liquid marquee events while thousands of markets have no sophisticated participation at all.

Sports Median Spread
100%

Sports drives 89% of Kalshi's $260M in 2025 revenue, yet the median market has a 100% spread. Volume is concentrated in marquee events. The long tail is completely unserved.

Science/Tech Mean vs Median
19% vs 51%

Median spread of 19% but mean of 51% — a bimodal distribution with a liquid core of well-followed tech events and a long tail of illiquid niche markets dragging the average up.

Efficiency Gap
71pp

71 percentage point gap between the most liquid category (Politics, 30%) and the least (Sports, 100%). In traditional financial markets this gap would be measured in basis points, not percentage points.

Widest Single Market
195%

The widest market in today's dataset: "How many consecutive games will Shai Gilgeous-Alexander score 20+ points?" Bid: 1¢. Ask: 98¢. A market in name only.

Tightest Single Market
0%

Trump/Greenland: bid and ask both at 30¢. Zero spread. Sophisticated participants have fully arbitraged this market. The contrast with Sports illustrates exactly where capital needs to go.

The API

QUERYABLE.
FILTERABLE.
REAL-TIME.

Six endpoints covering category summaries, per-market detail, historical trends, and spread extremes. Designed for quantitative integration, not dashboards.

GET
/v1/categories
Mean, median, min, max spread by category
GET
/v1/markets
All markets — filter by category, spread range, sort by any field
GET
/v1/markets/:ticker
Single market with full spread history over time
GET
/v1/history
Daily category spread averages — configurable lookback
GET
/v1/extremes
Widest and tightest markets right now, filterable by category
GET
/v1/status
Dataset health, row counts, timestamp range
